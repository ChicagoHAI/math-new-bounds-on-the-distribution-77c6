\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main_gap}
For sufficiently large $x$, we have:
\[\maxgap(x) \leq (\log x)^2 \cdot (\log\log x)^{-1} + O\left(\frac{(\log x)^2}{(\log\log x)^2}\right)\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof proceeds in three steps:

1) First, we apply the sieve of Eratosthenes to show that the density of primes in intervals of length $(\log x)^2$ is approximately $1/\log x$.

2) Using the Prime Number Theorem with error term, we can show that the probability of a "gap" longer than our bound is extremely small.
